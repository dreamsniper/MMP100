Prologue

She sat in a nearly empty E train deep in the tunnels of New York City's subways waiting to reach Columbus circle station. She was still and calm, with a countenance of cold composure. Her round lips curled in a sneer of disgust as she watched the man across from her. A sloppy foul looking man concerned with little else but devouring the greasy chips in front of him. Pounding them into his mouth as he gorges on more before he can even finish his last bite. Breathing heavily from the exertion of like his life depended on it

Oksana looked absolutely disgusted as he reached into the bag and grabbed the chips by the fistful, then wiping his hands on his already heavily greased stained pants. He looked up at her for a second and quietly looked away trying to avoid her stare. She saw shame in his eyes, but it wasn't shame at his public behavior, the look he gave her was the look one would give a beggar upon the streets.

"Gluttonous pig, can't you even wait until you've finished swallowing before you stuff the next round into your mouth?"

Oksana muttered to herself. In her disgust she stood up and started to walk to the other side of the train. Her long slender body glided across the floor as if she were more snake than human. Her long dark limp hair flowed as if a breeze passed right by her in the stillness of the train car. Oksana was no plain Jane by any means; some would say she was a model. Still one would think that such a striking creature like Oksana with her black macabre satin dress, five inch stilettos and milky white skin would command more attention than she was getting.

Oksana stopped and stood in front of the doors as the train pulled into her station. She took a quick glance around, almost as if she were looking out for something or someone. The train doors opened but not just the ones we would expect to open, but the door that she stood in front of which faced the wall also and no one else seemed to notice. What appeared on the other side was a dark winding staircase that looked ancient beyond the years of the actual train system like something out of a medieval castle.

Oksana quietly stepped into the quiet darkness and as the doors closed and the train moved on to its next destination only a blank wall remained where the door to nowhere had opened for Oksana Velour
